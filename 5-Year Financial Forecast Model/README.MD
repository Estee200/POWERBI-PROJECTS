## Project Overview

This project presents an interactive **5-Year Financial Forecast Dashboard** developed for **MedIn Technologies** to support a ₦150 Million investor funding proposal. The dashboard transforms detailed financial projections into an executive report, enabling investors to evaluate projected revenue growth, profitability, financial health, and funding requirements over a five-year period.

---

## Business Challenge

Early-stage startups seeking investment need to demonstrate a credible, data-backed growth story — not just ambition. MedIn Technologies needed to answer key investor questions in one place:

- How much funding is required, and what will it be used for?
- How fast will revenue and profit grow over the next 5 years?
- When does the business become profitable (break-even)?
- Which products will drive the most revenue?
- How strong is the company's financial position (assets, equity, liabilities)?

Without a consolidated forecast, communicating this story to investors would rely on scattered spreadsheets rather than a clear, navigable narrative.

## Investor Insights

- Revenue is projected to grow from ₦91M in Year 1 to ₦417M by Year 5 — a **360.8% increase**, driven primarily by subscription products, which contribute the largest share of projected revenue.
- The business reaches **break-even in Year 3**, giving investors a clear timeline for when the company moves from cash-consuming to self-sustaining.
- Net profit margin improves to **24.9%** by Year 5, with gross margin holding steady at **45.7%**, indicating the business scales efficiently rather than sacrificing margin for growth.
- MedIn Orbit is the dominant revenue driver at ₦788M, far outpacing MedIn Subs, MedIn Linq, and MedIn Care — suggesting future investment should prioritize scaling Orbit while evaluating the smaller product lines' long-term viability.
- The waterfall breakdown shows COGS (-₦659.2M) as the largest deduction from revenue, followed by Overheads and Variables, before arriving at a Year 5 net profit of ₦279.1M — giving investors visibility into cost structure, not just top-line growth.
- The company's financial position remains strong throughout the forecast, with Total Assets reaching ₦1.0BN against Total Liabilities of just ₦134.4M, reflecting low leverage and a healthy equity base of ₦890.5M.

---

## Recommendations

- Prioritize investment in high-performing products to maximize long-term returns.
- Monitor actual performance against forecast assumptions and adjust business strategies where necessary.
- Maintain cost discipline to protect projected profit margins as the business grows.
- Provide investors with regular financial updates to demonstrate progress against the forecast.
